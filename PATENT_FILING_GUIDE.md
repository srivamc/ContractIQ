## Phase 1: Indian Patent Office (IPO) Filing

### Step 1.1: Prepare Provisional Specification (Month 0)

**Documents to Prepare:**
1. **Form 1** - Application for grant of patent
   - Applicant details (name, address, nationality)
   - Title: "Autonomous Multi-Layer Agentic Framework for Contract-Driven Software Quality Engineering"
   - Field of invention: Computer Science, AI, Software Testing
   
2. **Form 2** - Provisional Specification
   - **Title**: Same as Form 1
   - **Technical Field**: Software test automation, AI, autonomous systems
   - **Background**: Problems with current testing frameworks
   - **Summary**: ContractIQ solution overview
   - **Description**: Detailed 3-layer architecture
   - **Claims** (if provisional complete): Not required for provisional

**Online Filing Process:**
1. Login to https://ipindiaonline.gov.in/
2. Navigate to "e-Filing" → "Patent" → "New Application"
3. Upload Form 1 (PDF)
4. Upload Form 2 - Provisional Specification (PDF)
5. Pay fee: ₹1,600 (Startup), ₹8,000 (Small Entity), ₹32,000 (Large Entity)
6. Submit with DSC
7. **Save Application Number** - This is your priority reference!

**Timeline**: 1 day to 1 week  
**Cost**: ₹1,600 (Startup)

---

### Step 1.2: File Complete Specification (Month 6-12)

Within 12 months of provisional filing, file complete specification with detailed claims.

**Additional Requirements:**
- **Form 2** - Complete Specification
  - Detailed description (20-40 pages recommended)
  - **Claims** (15-25 claims typical):
    - Independent claims (3-5): Core inventions
    - Dependent claims (10-20): Specific implementations
  - **Drawings**: Architecture diagrams, flowcharts, sequence diagrams
  - **Abstract**: 150 words summary

**Key Sections to Include:**
1. **3-Layer Agent Architecture** (Management, Specialist, Foundation)
2. **Contract-as-Truth Engine** with spec parsing
3. **MCP-Integrated Intelligent Memory**
4. **Autonomous Root Cause Analysis (ARCA)**
5. **Predictive Self-Healing & Refactoring**

**Filing Process:**
- Same as provisional, but upload Complete Specification
- Reference provisional application number
- Pay fee if not paid with provisional

**Timeline**: 1-2 weeks preparation + filing  
**Cost**: ₹1,600 (if separate from provisional)

---

### Step 1.3: Request for Examination (Month 12-48)

**Form 18** - Request for Examination (RFE)

**When to File:**
- Mandatory within **48 months** of priority date
- Recommended: File within 12-18 months for faster processing
- Startups get expedited examination (1-2 years vs 3-4 years)

**Process:**
1. Login to IPO portal
2. Navigate to application → "Request for Examination"
3. Upload Form 18
4. Pay examination fee: ₹4,000 (Startup), ₹20,000 (Small), ₹80,000 (Large)
5. Submit

**Timeline**: 1 day  
**Cost**: ₹4,000 (Startup)

---

### Step 1.4: Publication (Month 18)

- **Automatic**: IPO publishes application 18 months after priority date
- **Early Publication** (Optional): File Form 9 for early publication
  - Benefit: Establishes public record, deters copycats
  - Cost: ₹2,500 (Startup)

**No action required unless requesting early publication**

---

### Step 1.5: First Examination Report (FER) - Month 24-36

IPO examiner reviews application and issues FER with:
- Objections (novelty, inventive step, clarity)
- Prior art citations
- Required amendments

**Response Required:**
- **Form 13** - Reply to FER
- **Deadline**: 6 months from FER date (extendable by 3 months with fee)

**What to Include in Response:**
1. Address each objection point-by-point
2. Amend claims if necessary (narrow scope to distinguish from prior art)
3. Provide arguments for novelty and inventive step
4. Cite differences from prior art
5. May need professional patent agent help

**Timeline**: 6 months to prepare response  
**Cost**: Professional agent fees ₹30,000-₹60,000

---

### Step 1.6: Hearing (If Required) - Month 30-42

If examiner not satisfied with response:
- IPO schedules hearing at patent office
- Present arguments, demonstrate invention
- May amend claims further

**Preparation:**
- Prepare presentation deck
- Bring technical documentation
- Patent agent representation recommended

**Timeline**: 1-2 hours hearing  
**Cost**: Travel + agent fees

---

### Step 1.7: Grant & Publication - Month 36-60

Once examiner satisfied:
- Patent granted
- Published in Indian Patent Journal
- **Patent Certificate** issued

**Post-Grant Requirements:**
1. **Annual Renewal Fees**: Starting from Year 3
   - Years 3-6: ₹800/year (Startup)
   - Years 7-10: ₹2,400/year
   - Years 11-15: ₹4,800/year
   - Years 16-20: ₹8,000/year

2. **Working Statement (Form 27)**: Annual declaration
   - Due every year from 1st March to 31st March
   - Declare whether patent is "worked" in India
   - Penalty for non-filing: Patent may be revoked

**Total Indian Patent Cost**: ₹40,000-₹90,000 (with agent)

## Phase 2: International Filing Strategy

### Option A: PCT (Patent Cooperation Treaty) Route

**Recommended for**: 5+ target countries

#### PCT Step 1: File International Application (Month 12)

**File within 12 months of Indian priority date**

**Documents Required:**
- PCT Request Form (PCT/RO/101)
- Description, Claims, Abstract, Drawings (same as Indian complete spec)
- Claim Indian priority

**Filing Options:**
1. **Through IPO as Receiving Office (RO)**
   - Transmittal fee: ₹8,000
   - International filing fee: CHF 1,330 (~₹1,20,000)
   - Search fee: ₹40,000-₹80,000 (depends on ISA - Indian, European, Korean)

2. **Directly with WIPO** (not recommended - more expensive)

**Total Cost**: ₹1,70,000-₹2,10,000

**Timeline**: 1-2 weeks filing

---

#### PCT Step 2: International Search Report (ISR) - Month 16

- International Searching Authority (ISA) conducts prior art search
- Issues ISR with patentability opinion
- Review to understand global prior art

**No action required - just review report**

---

#### PCT Step 3: International Publication - Month 18

- PCT application published by WIPO
- Publicly accessible worldwide

**No action required**

---

#### PCT Step 4: National Phase Entry - Month 30-31

**Critical Deadline**: Enter national phase in target countries

**Target Countries (typical selection):**
1. **United States** (USPTO)
   - Cost: $5,000-$8,000 (filing + translation + agent)
   - Large market, strong IP protection
   
2. **Europe** (EPO - covers 38 countries)
   - Cost: €5,000-€8,000 (~₹4,50,000-₹7,20,000)
   - Designate countries (DE, FR, GB, NL common)
   
3. **China** (CNIPA)
   - Cost: $3,000-$5,000 + translation ₹50,000
   - Fast-growing AI/testing market
   
4. **Japan** (JPO)
   - Cost: $4,000-$6,000 + translation ₹40,000
   - High-tech market

**Per Country Cost**: $5,000-$15,000  
**Total for 4-5 countries**: $20,000-$50,000 (₹16,00,000-₹40,00,000)

---

### Option B: Paris Convention Direct Filing

**Recommended for**: 2-4 target countries

**Process:**
- File directly in target patent offices within 12 months
- Claim Indian priority
- Similar costs to PCT national phase

**Pros:**
- Skip PCT intermediary (saves PCT fees)
- Faster examination in some countries

**Cons:**
- No international search report
- More upfront costs
- Less flexibility

## Forms Checklist

- [ ] **Form 1**: Application for grant of patent
- [ ] **Form 2**: Provisional or Complete Specification

- [ ] **Form 18**: Request for examination

## Professional Help & Resources

### When to Hire a Patent Agent
**Recommended for:**
- FER response (critical for patent grant)
- Claim drafting (for complete specification)
- International filings (complex procedures)

**Not Required for:**
- Provisional filing (straightforward)
- RFE filing (simple form)

### Finding Patent Agents
- **Indian Patent Office List**: https://ipindia.gov.in/registered-patent-agents.htm
- **Bangalore-based agents**: Search for "patent agent Bangalore"
- **Cost**: ₹30,000-₹1,00,000 depending on complexity

### Government Resources
- **IPO Portal**: https://ipindiaonline.gov.in/
- **Startup India IP**: https://www.startupindia.gov.in/content/sih/en/ipo-incentive.html
  - 50% rebate on examination fees
  - Fast-track examination
- **Patent Facilitation Centers**: Free consultations
  - IIT Madras PFC: https://pfc.iitm.ac.in/
  - NSTEDB-TIFAC: pan-India centers

### Learning Resources
- **IPO Manual of Patent Office Practice**: https://ipindia.gov.in/manual-for-patent-office-practice-and-procedure.htm
- **WIPO PCT Applicant's Guide**: https://www.wipo.int/pct/en/appguide/
